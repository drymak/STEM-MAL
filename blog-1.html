DO NOT LET PUBLIC SPEAKING PARALYZE YOU AT THE CONFERENCE

  • Admin
  • Sep 1, 2020
  • Standard
  • No Comments

Plato once said, “A wise man speaks because he has something to say; a fool speaks because he has to say something”. ¬Everyone fears public speaking at some point, but nothing can defeat passion. If you have been participating in MAL for a while, you have the power, the passion to speak and deliver your idea! It can be stressful to exist in a medium full of seasoned delegates who dedicate most of their time to MAL, but, most importantly, you aren’t there to be the smartest one in the hall; you just have to persuade, engage in the process and enjoy the competitive spirit.

The secret to defeat this fear is to practice and prepare. As was said, practice makes perfect. Always have an idea of what you’re going to say, whether you write it down or illustrate it in your imagination. Practice every time you have a chance to practice. Use your secret weapon in such situations, the mirror.

There’s no way to absolutely prevent tripping over your words or forgetting what you want to say, but that’s okay. For the first time experiencing MAL, many delegates face this kind of fear. Find others who were delegates or have done MAL before, and talk to them about it. Over time, you’ll realize that it is easy to return to path in MAL, as long as you pay attention and concentrate. So, organize your ideas, your words, and materials, that makes you calmer and more relaxed, and then, you will stay focused and you will deliver a great speech.

In order to give the best speech, you have to first defeat Fear of Rejection. “What if they boo me off the floor?” Eliminate all fear of rejection. Model Arab league is a supportive community and understand that everyone has their first time. Do not be afraid; facing your fear is not only the way to learn but also a part of the game. Second, watch yourself in the mirror. Once your speech flows naturally out of your mouth see how you look. Try to practice your speech in front of a mirror as if you were speaking to an audience as was mentioned. Specifically, pay attention to your facial expressions, your body language, your gestures, and your posture. And don’t worry if your speech is not perfect. After all, not everyone has a loud booming voice or a Hollywood-lawyer-style charisma.

Try to stay relaxed and eliminate anxiety, because when you are comfortable and pandering to your strengths you will be most authentic, and others will want to listen more.

Don’t be afraid to pause. Some delegates naturally rush through their speech to get in as much as possible. In contrast, the right speech pause might be more powerful than the best material rushed through. As a bonus, pausing will not only increase your effectiveness but also give you time to semi relax, recompose yourself and breath. “Some say the dramatic pauses is so powerful that they should be illegal.” Brian Tracy

Types of Pauses:
The emphatic pause – Used to emphasize an important point
The dramatic pause: give a point time to stick in listeners minds
The sense pause: allows people to both absorb new information and catch up with you
The sentence-completion pause: Used by making a statement, or quote, which everyone knows and letting the other delegates complete it for you.

FINALLY, IN SUMMARY, REMEMBER TO HAVE FUN! MAL is a life changing experience, don’t let the anxiety and fear paralyze you. When you join MAL, you never stop learning and seeking knowledge through different outlets. At the end of the day, every delegate can tell you that it was never just an after-school activity, but a pathway that taught us awareness, diplomacy, and confidence.
