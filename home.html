Our story

The STEM Model Arab League is a working group of a team of young people that aims to discuss issues in the Arab world based on what is happening in the eastern region. We also aim to encourage young people to speak about the freedom of their opinion and the complete independence of the Arab peoples intellectually and practically. The STEM Model Arab League also seeks to train Arab youth freely without the influence of any third parties.

The Arab League

The “League of Arab States” with its 22 members, is a regional organization with the objective of invigorating the relations between Arab countries, coordinating collaboration projects, and conserving the peace and sovereignty of the member states. With that said, the Arab league has played great roles in several events such as the Arab spring, gulf war, Sudan crisis, and countless others.

As for the pyramidal order in the Arab League, every country has a specific number of representatives to show and defend their countries point of view on various events, and while those representatives have high diplomatic prestige they are not the head of the organization, the highest rank is the general secretary. His duties include supervising all the administrative and political functions of the organization, follow up the implementation of the resolutions of the Council, adjust the date of the sessions and meetings and make reports and declarations on the subjects discussed by the Council. The individual bearing this responsibility is replaced every 5 years.

The Arab League wasn’t always what it is today actually when it was established only 6 Arabian countries were official members and through the years from 1945 to this day it has been expanding until all 22 Arab countries have joined the council and not only the member count was expanding, but so was its responsibilities.

Objectives

Our Mission

• Help students learn, train, and work, as well as political and public affairs activists.

• Help learners regardless of the career or profession they elect to pursue.

• Spread the prestigious history of the Arab world with young Egyptian minds using our social media presence.

• Provide the opportunity for students to network, collaborate, and dispute in a diplomatic setting.

As longtime MAL Program Coordinators have noted, “We’re providing future leaders the experience of being in the shoes of, and having to deal with, someone they might never understand or be able to deal with effectively otherwise.”

Our Vision

• Act as a voice to help share the Arab problems with the world.

• Push our students to be knowledgeable, well-trained citizens as well as civic and public affairs leaders by stimulating resolutions and debate issues in the Middle East related to social affairs, political matters, defense concerns, and the environment.

• Create a generation of future politicians and problem-solvers who are well-knowledgeable of world issues and well-informed about international relations.
